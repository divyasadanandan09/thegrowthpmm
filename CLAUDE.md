# TheGrowthPMM — marketing site

Single-page marketing site for **Divya Sadanandan** ("TheGrowthPMM"), an independent **product marketing / growth consultant**. Built with **Astro + Tailwind v4**, but most styling is hand-authored inline styles + a custom design system in `src/styles/global.css` (CSS variables, not Tailwind utilities).

## Run / verify
- Dev server: `npm run dev` (Astro, default port 4321).
- Prefer the `preview_*` tools to verify visually. Note: in past sessions, deep-scroll screenshots sometimes came back blank — verify layout via DOM (`getBoundingClientRect`, computed styles) when screenshots are unreliable, and reserve screenshots for the hero/top of page.

## Key files
- `src/pages/index.astro` — the whole homepage (nav, hero, logo strip, problem, services, how-it-works, proof, testimonials, why-me comparison, about, the free report as a soft section, closing CTA, footer).
- `src/pages/faq.astro` — separate FAQ page at `/faq` (six Q&As in a `<details>` accordion), linked from footer + mobile menu. Carries `FAQPage` JSON-LD.
- `src/pages/ai-product-marketing.astro` — AEO / thought-leadership pillar at `/ai-product-marketing` (AI PMM point of view, the workflows, guardrails, proof, a 4-Q FAQ). The "leading voice of AI PMM" content home. Linked from footer + mobile menu. Carries `FAQPage` JSON-LD.
- `src/styles/global.css` — design system: navy palette CSS vars, `.handwritten` (Caveat accents + `.light` variant), `.sticky-tag`, `.module` (Services cards), `.channel-grid`, `.compare-*` (Why-me: alternatives get ✗, `.compare-card--me` gets ✓), `.process-*` (How it works steps), `.testimonial-lead` + `.testimonial-pair` (editorial testimonials: a featured pull-quote with an oversized Caveat quote mark, then two borderless recs under a hairline — not cards, changed 2026-06-29), `.steps-flow` + `.report-checklist` (report card), `.funnel-*`, responsive rules. (`.service-row`, `.marker`, `.audit-step` linger but are unused.)
- `src/layouts/Layout.astro` — `<head>`, fonts (Inter + Caveat), meta/OG, and the global **JSON-LD entity graph** (`Person` Divya Sadanandan + `ProfessionalService` TheGrowthPMM) emitted on every page so answer engines resolve the entity.

## Positioning & strategy (the "why", not obvious from code)
- **The site is call-led** (changed 2026-06-24; reverses the earlier lead-magnet-led approach — see `HANDOFF.md`). The primary conversion action everywhere is **booking a call**. Primary CTA text is **"Book a call"**; keep it consistent. Why: Divya takes only four clients a month, so good-fit calls beat list volume.
- The **free Growth Audit** (renamed from "Growth Report" 2026-06-26; a Google Form → personalized audit by email) is the **secondary, soft option** in the "not ready for a call yet?" section placed late on the page (after About, before the closing band). Nav link + CTAs read "Growth audit" / "Get FREE growth audit". Don't promote it back to primary without intent.
- The **hero is the pitch**, **outcome-led** (copy refreshed 2026-07-01, headline shortened again later that day). H1 is now "Unlock the next growth stage." (the lime `.marker` highlights "next growth stage"; the old second sentence "I identify what's holding growth back and fix it." was cut, and its idea moved onto the Services H2, see below). The follow-up is a single **body-sized** paragraph that reads as one thought ("I work with founders and teams who have users, traction, and ambition but aren't growing fast enough. Sometimes the fix is sharper positioning, better activation, a tighter GTM, or a stronger launch. I start with the growth problem, then work backward to what will move it."). Tag: a single **"Only 3 slots per month"** (swapped 2026-07-01 from "Top 100 Global PMM, 2025" — the award tag read oddly floating alone above the H1; scarcity ties directly into the CTA instead. The "Fractional Head of PMM" tag was dropped earlier). Single CTA "Book a call" plus a reassurance line ("A 20-minute call, no pitch. You'll leave with a clear first move.").
- **Services H2** is "I fix what's holding your business back." (shortened 2026-07-01 from "I fix the part of growth that's holding your business back."). **Line-wrap fix (same day):** several sub-heads/headings were wrapping to two lines only because of narrow inline `max-width`/`font-size`, not because the copy was too long — widened the Services subhead `max-width` 620px→1050px, dropped the About H2 font-size 40px→36px (its two-col `1.55fr` column is narrower than a full-width section), and widened the right-fit intro wrapper `max-width` 600px→850px. All three now render on one line at desktop widths; don't re-narrow without re-checking.
- The **"leak" metaphor was retired site-wide** (2026-06-25). Use **"drop-off" / "where growth stalls" / "losing users" / "the constraint"** instead. The funnel visual stays (caption is now "Where are you losing users?"); don't reintroduce the word "leak" in copy.
- **CTA placement:** the floating corner "Book a call" carries the call through mid-page. Inline "Book a call" CTAs now sit in the hero, after **How it works**, after **Testimonials**, and in the closing band (added 2026-06-25 per council; this reverses the earlier "no mid-page inline CTA" rule). - **ICP:** founders / CEOs / co-founders of **post-PMF startups**, funded in the last 6–12 months, with first paying customers but **stalled growth**. Copy should address them directly.
- Services are **modular, not a fixed 1-2-3 sequence** (changed 2026-06-25). **Growth strategy is the featured/hero service** (most engagements start there; keeps the 4-card channel grid: paid / organic / lifecycle / experimentation). **Positioning & messaging audit** and **Go-to-market** are presented below as secondary "other services" (condensed, no sub-grids). Framing: "I plug in where your team needs me, starting with growth," not "I do all three in order."

## Design system
- **LIME REBRAND (2026-07-01) — the palette is now lime + near-black, NOT navy. Don't revert to navy.** `--navy` is now the lime pop `#DBFF00` (the token kept its name to avoid renaming ~everywhere; it's a misnomer). Lime is only ever used as a **fill** (buttons, badge chips, the featured-card border) with **dark `--ink` text on top**, or as an **accent on the dark surfaces** (`.handwritten.light` cursive, the closing-band CTA). Lime is **never** text on a light background (unreadable, ~1.1:1). The dark surfaces that used to be navy (footer, closing band, Growth Audit card, recognition band, `.compare-card--me`) now use the new **`--ink-deep` #16180f** near-black. Accent text on light sections (headings' sublines, the big proof numbers, eyebrows, `.handwritten`) is dark `--ink`/`--ink-deep`, not lime. `.btn-navy`/`#navbar .btn-navy` text is `--ink` (was #fff); `::selection`, focus rings and the hamburger icon are dark, not lime. The `<Logo>` mark and `favicon.svg` are now a **lime `#DBFF00` rounded-square badge** with a near-black `#16180f` funnel + coral `#cc4117` dot (recolored 2026-07-01; the mark used to be a bare dark funnel — this brings the lime fill into the wordmark). The Logo `reverse` variant now only swaps the wordmark colour to white; the badge stays lime on both light nav and dark footer. See `HANDOFF.md` 2026-07-01 for the full token map.
- **Backgrounds:** cool navy-tinted neutrals (`--cream` #e7ebf5, `--blue-tint`, white; warm beige retired 2026-06-29). `--warm-line` #d6dcec is the cool hairline on tinted sections.
- **Type (updated 2026-07-01):** **Bricolage Grotesque** is the display face for all headings via `--font-display` (`h1,h2,h3`); **Inter** stays for body/UI; **Caveat** (`.handwritten`) for playful scrapbook accents. Coral (`#cc4117` logo dot, `#ec6a43` funnel drop-off balls) is the one supporting accent, meaning "drop-off". So the system is **lime + near-black + coral**.
- **Sticky tags are now the lime/near-black pair (2026-07-01):** `.sticky-tag.purple` = lime fill + dark text, `.sticky-tag.gold` = near-black fill + lime text (class names kept, hues retired). On the near-black recognition band the tag uses `.purple` (lime) so it stays visible.
- Playful elements (handwritten accents, rotated `.sticky-tag` notes, grid-canvas) are intentional. The **handwritten kicker was thinned 2026-07-01** — it no longer sits above nearly every section (dropped on Services/Proof/Testimonials; kept on How-it-works, About, right-fit, Report, closing). The `.marker` highlighter is **now lime and in use** on "next growth stage" (hero H1) and "the numbers" (proof H2) to carry the brand colour through the light sections.
- The hero **funnel is a 3D SVG** (stacked frustums with elliptical rims + cylindrical shading), **retinted 2026-07-01** from purple→navy→teal to a **dark body (`#3c4a1c`→`#22271a`→`#14170e`) with lime rim contours + a lime top-glow + a coral spout-glow**; white stage labels stay readable on the dark body. Balls are animated inline via **SVG/SMIL** (`<animateMotion>`): **lime** balls flow down and converge to the spout, **coral** balls drop out at different stages. (Not the old CSS `.funnel-dot`/`.funnel-drip`, which were removed.)
- **Mobile:** the floating corner "Book a call" (`#nav-cta-corner`) **docks to the bottom** (`bottom:16px`) below 900px — thumb zone, and it no longer overlaps section headings scrolling under the top-right corner (fixed 2026-07-01).
- **Services** render as one featured `.module--featured` card (Growth strategy, with a "where most teams start" sticky-tag) plus an `.other-services` 2-up grid (Positioning audit, Go-to-market (GTM) strategy). No numbers. The **About** section: bio on the left with a right-aligned photo (grid is `1.55fr 0.45fr`, `align-items:start` — the bio gets the room). **Photo treatment redone 2026-07-01 (`/impeccable polish`):** the old plain rounded-rect (`border-radius:18px`) is now a circular **medallion** — `.about-photo-frame` (240px, lime `--navy` fill as a halo ring, `box-shadow: 0 20px 44px rgba(22,24,15,.22)`) wrapping `.about-photo` (circular crop, `object-position:50% 22%`, 3px `--ink-deep` inner ring). Mobile (`<900px`): 168px, left-aligned. It closes with an **"Is this the right fit?"** qualifier block (replaced the full-time-vs-Growth-PMM comparison, 2026-06-30; **layout revisited 2026-07-01** from a narrow centered island to a **left-aligned** block matching the site's section rhythm — a "let's be honest" handwritten eyebrow into the heading, then a 2-col `.compare-grid` at `max-width:900px`, cards ~442px each). Cards reuse the compare-card styles — near-black `--ink-deep` "A fit if" card (green ✓, 4 items) + white "Not a fit if" card (red ✗, 3 items). It qualifies the ICP honestly and filters bad-fit calls. Deliberately drops a "regulated industry" disqualifier (would contradict the Fi Money/healthtech proof) and any hours/price framing.
