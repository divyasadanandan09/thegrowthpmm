## Register

brand

## Users

Founders, CEOs, and co-founders of **post-PMF B2B SaaS startups** — typically funded in the last 6–12 months, with first paying customers but **stalled growth**. They arrive weighing whether to hire a full-time senior PMM or bring in fractional help, and they're skeptical of consultant fluff. Job to be done: figure out what's actually capping their growth and whether this person can fix it, then decide whether to book a call. A junior marketer or an operator who needs senior direction is a secondary reader.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for **Divya Sadanandan ("The Growth PMM")**, a fractional Head of Product Marketing. The site is **call-led**: the single primary action everywhere is **booking a 20-minute call**. Divya takes only **three clients a month**, so the site optimizes for a few good-fit calls, not email-list volume. A **free Growth Audit** (Google Form → personalized diagnostic) is the deliberate soft, secondary path for people not ready to book. Success = qualified founders booking calls; the "is this the right fit?" block exists to filter out bad-fit ones.

## Brand Personality

Senior, confident, and plain-spoken — the voice of someone who has seen the pattern before and will tell you the truth, including "we're not a fit." Lightly playful through handwritten scrapbook accents (Caveat) used in moderation, never at the cost of clarity. Diagnostic, not salesy. Voice: **second person to the reader, first person ("I") for Divya**. Copy follows a strict anti-AI writing style — **zero em dashes**, no rule-of-three padding, no "not X but Y" parallelism, no empty consultant eyebrow phrases. Emotional goals: credibility, reassurance ("no pitch, you'll leave with a clear first move"), and the sense that she diagnoses before she prescribes.

## Anti-references

- AI-slop landing pages; anything where a visitor would say "AI made this."
- The **cream / beige / warm-neutral** near-white AI default (deliberately retired from this site).
- Generic SaaS templates: gradient hero-metric blocks, identical icon+heading+text card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section.
- AI-prose tells: em dashes, rule-of-three, "it's not X, it's Y," category-buzzword filler.
- Overpromising agency tone / hype ("10x your growth"). Honesty and qualification are the differentiator.

## Design Principles

- **One job, call-led.** Everything points to booking a call; the free audit is a soft fallback, never promoted to primary.
- **Honest qualification over hype.** The site filters bad-fit leads on purpose (the "a fit / not a fit" block); saying no builds trust.
- **Show the diagnosis, don't just claim it.** The funnel visual, real named numbers, and recognizable logos do the proving.
- **Playful in moderation, readable always.** Scrapbook accents and the lime pop are seasoning; clarity and contrast win every conflict.
- **Practice what you preach.** The site is itself a positioning artifact — if it can't demonstrate sharp positioning and craft, the pitch is hollow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text contrast ≥4.5:1 (large text ≥3:1); authored `:focus-visible` rings; a global `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback plus the hero funnel SMIL paused for reduced-motion users; footer/touch targets sized to WCAG 2.5.8; images carry alt text + width/height. The 2026-07-01 lime rebrand must preserve all of this: lime (`#DBFF00`) is only ever a fill with **dark** text on it, never text on light; white text lives only on the near-black `--ink-deep` surfaces.
